From Cabinets to Boardrooms

I started this career pulling cable and configuring switches, and I have never lost that hands-on instinct. Every framework I have since worked with — NIST, HISF, NZISF, ISO 27001 — is only useful if it changes what actually happens on the network and in the boardroom.

At Deloitte, I built a cybersecurity MSSP from nothing — the service design, the client relationships, the team. At Ember, I am doing the same thing at scale, across 200+ managed environments. The through-line in everything is the same: find the gap between where security is and where it needs to be, and close it in a way that actually holds.

Whether the conversation is with a healthcare CIO working through HISF compliance, a bank needing an independent security assessment, or an MSSP trying to turn ad-hoc security work into a scalable service line, the job is the same: make risk visible, make the response practical, and make sure it sticks.
